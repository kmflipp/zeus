A.   SUBJECT AND SCOPE OF INSURANCE AND GEOGRAPHICAL LIMITS

1.     The property specified in the Schedule of the policy or certificate is insured against loss or damage from whatsoever cause arising, except as hereinafter provided, during the period of Insurance, in Switzerland and Liechtenstein and during journeys and temporary stays within the geographical limits and up to the insured amounts specified in the Schedule.

2.     For articles separately specified and for which specific sums insured are shown in the List of Insured Objects or in a separate list forming part of the policy or certificate, these sums shall be considered as agreed values, in accordance with Art. 65 of the Federal Law concerning the Insurance Contract.

3.     Any item in the List of Insured Objects which covers articles not separately specified is subject to average, that is to say, if the value of all articles covered by such item shall at the time of any loss or damage be of greater value than the sum insured hereby in respect thereof, the Assured shall according to Art. 69 of the Federal Law concerning the Insurance Contract be entitled to recover hereunder only such proportion of the said loss or damage as the sum insured in respect of such items bears to the total value of all articles covered by such item.

Nevertheless should any item specified in the List of Insured Objects as Personal Effects be lost or damaged elsewhere than at the Assured's Private Dwelling House or Flat, then for the purpose of applying average, as above, no account shall be taken of the amount of the Assured's Personal Effects at the said Private Dwelling House or Flat at the time of the said loss or damage.

4.     Where any insured item consists of articles in a pair or set, this Insurance is not to pay more than the value of any particular part or parts which may be lost, without reference to any special value which such article or articles may have as part of such pair or set; nor more than a proportionate part of the insured value of the pair or set.

B.   EXCLUSIONS

5.   This Insurance does not cover;

1.     breakage of articles of a brittle nature, other than jewellery, unless such breakage is caused by burglars, thieves or fire;

2.     loss or damage caused by vermin of any kind or gradual deterioration;

3.     loss or damage caused by wear and tear, or electrical or mechanical failure other than loss or damage resulting from wear and tear or mechanical failure of a clasp, setting or other fastening, carrier or container;

4.     damage to or deterioration of any article caused by the actual process of dyeing, cleaning, repair or renovation.

5.     breakage of glass, overwinding, denting or internal damage of clocks or watches of any kind;

6.     loss of cash, currency or bank notes;

7.     loss of, or damage to jewellery whilst on the premises of Hotels or Motels UNLESS the said jewellery is being worn by the Assured, or is contained in a locked safe or bank vault.

8.     loss of jewellery from baggage UNLESS carried by hand and under the personal supervision of the Assured.

9.     any loss or damage in respect of which the Assured fails to comply with Underwriter's request to inform the police immediately and to demand an official investigation.

10.   (a)   loss or destruction of or damage to any property whatsoever or any loss or expense

whatsoever resulting or arising therefrom or any consequential loss,

(b)   any legal liability of whatsoever nature,

directly or indirectly caused by or contributed to by or arising from

1.     ionising radiation or contamination by radioactivity from any nuclear fuel or from any nuclear waste from the combustion of nuclear fuel.

2.     the radioactive, toxic, explosive or other hazardous properties of any explosive nuclear assembly or nuclear component thereof.

11.   loss or damage directly or indirectly occasioned by, happening through or in consequence of war, invasion, acts of foreign enemies, hostilities (whether war be declared or not), civil war, rebellion, revolution, insurrection, military or usurped power or confiscation or nationalisation or requisition or destruction of or damage to property by or under the order of any government or public or local authority.

C.   GENERAL CONDITIONS

6.   Change of residence

The Assured shall notify the Underwriters, within 14 days, of any change of residence.

7.   Non-disclosure

If the Assured on the conclusion or renewal of this Insurance shall have made a false declaration of a material fact which he knew or ought to have known, or if he omitted to declare such fact, the Underwriters are entitled to cancel the Insurance in accordance with Art. 6 of the Federal Law concerning the insurance contract if they give notice thereof to the Assured within 4 weeks of such false declaration or omission coming to their knowledge.

Non-disclosure on conclusion or renewal entitles Underwriters to such cancellation during all subsequent renewal periods.

8.   Modification of Risk

If during the course of this Insurance the alteration of material facts causes an increase in the risk, immediate notice thereof shall be given to the Underwriters in writing.

If the Assured has taken any active part in materially increasing the risk, the Underwriters shall be freed from liability for loss or damage.  They shall be entitled to cancel the Insurance with immediate effect.

If the risk has been materially increased without the Assured having taken any active part therein, the Underwriters shall also be freed from liability for loss or damage, and shall be entitled to cancel the Insurance with immediate effect, if the Assured fails to notify them in writing of the increase of the risk immediately it comes to his notice.

The Underwriters shall also be entitled to cancel the Insurance even if the Assured has properly notified the increase of the risk. In such cases Underwriters' liability shall expire 14 days after they have notified the Assured of the cancellation of the Insurance.

If the Insurance is cancelled prior to expiration following an increase of risk or is continued at a reduced level of risk, a pro rata return of premium paid for the current insurance period shall be made.

9.   Changing Hands

1.     If the insured property in its entirety changes hands, the Insurance shall remain in force.  The vendor is obliged to give immediate notice in writing to the Underwriters of the effected transfer of property. The Underwriters shall be entitled to cancel the Insurance within 14 days of their being notified of the transfer of the property, subject to the return of a proportionate amount of the premium. Underwriters' liability shall expire 4 weeks after they have given written notice of cancellation to the Assured.

2.     This Insurance will not be transferred to the acquirer if:

1.     by his acquiring the property a material increase of risk results and if Underwriters cancel in writing the Insurance within 14 days after their being notified of the change of hands;

2.     if the acquirer notifies Underwriters in writing of his cancellation of the Insurance within 14 days after the change of hands.

10.  Obligations in Case of Loss or Damage

The Assured has the following obligations, any inexcusable breach of which forfeits the right of claim:

1.     to give immediate notification of a claim to Underwriters;

2.     to give full information to Underwriters in writing about the cause, extent and detailed circumstances of the loss, allow Underwriters to make all necessary investigations and co-operate with them and the police;

3.     to take all appropriate measures for the reduction of the damage and the preservation of the Insured Objects and to follow instructions given by the Underwriters to this end;

4.     not to remove any traces which might prejudice the assessment of the cause and extent of the loss or damage;

5.     to advise Underwriters immediately if objects which have disappeared are returned or if information is received about them.

11.  Fraudulent Claims

If the Assured or a Beneficiary makes any claim knowing the same to be false or fraudulent as regards the amount or otherwise, the Underwriters shall be released from all liability in respect of all claims made under this insurance by that claimant.

12.  Payment of Claims

1.     In the event of loss, the Underwriters shall be entitled, at their sole option, to replace, either wholly or in part, any lost article or to make a cash payment not exceeding the corresponding insured value therefor.

2.     Claims shall be paid at the domicile of the Assured or Assignee and shall become due for payment four weeks after the loss has been proved as provided by Article 41 of the Federal Law concerning the Insurance Contract.

3.     Payment of a claim, however, does not become due as long as:

1.     there is uncertainty about the beneficiary entitled to receive payment;

2.     a police or criminal investigation is pending in respect of the claim event and proceedings against the Assured or Assignee have not been concluded.

4.     If the Assured subsequently recovers articles in respect of which he has been indemnified, he shall at his option either repay the Underwriters, after making deduction for any loss of value, or surrender the recovered property to the Underwriters.

 

 

13.  Cancellation in Case of Claim

This Insurance may be cancelled after any claim for which indemnity has been paid by the Underwriters, either

-       by the Assured not later than 14 days after having received payment,

or

-       by the Underwriters not later than at the time of payment.

The premium for the current period of Insurance belongs to the Underwriters.  If the Underwriters cancel, their liability shall expire 4 weeks after receipt of their cancellation notice by the Assured and Underwriters shall make a return of premium in respect of the remaining sum insured for the unexpired period of Insurance.

14.  Adjustment of Premium

If the rating scale is altered, Underwriters may request adjustment of the premium from the next annual insurance period. For this purpose Underwriters shall advise the Assured of the new premium at the latest 30 days before it becomes due.  The Assured is thereupon entitled to cancel the Insurance at the end of the current annual period. If cancelled by the Assured the entire Insurance shall expire at the end of the annual period of insurance.  The notice of cancellation shall be valid only if it reaches Underwriters or their authorised  representatives at the latest on the last day of the annual period of insurance.  Failure by the Assured to cancel shall be deemed to be acceptance of the adjustment of the Insurance to be new rate.

15.  Statute of Limitations / Forfeiture

Claims arising from this Insurance are time-barred two years after the event giving rise to the claim. Repudiated claims expire two years after the event if no court action has been commenced.

16.  Notice

All notices which may be required to be sent by the Assured or the Assignee to Underwriters shall be served in writing on the address contained in this policy or certificate or subsequently brought to the knowledge of the Assured or Assignee or at the Seat of Administration for the entire Swiss Business.  All notices addressed by Underwriters to the Assured or Assignee shall be served on the address last known to Underwriters.

17.  Litigation

Legal actions for the full amount of a claim may be directed against Underwriters subscribing to this Insurance.  The defendant Underwriters have therein to be named "Lloyd's Underwriters, London, subscribing to contract  QUOTEORPOLICYNUMBER  represented by their Mandataire General for Switzerland".

18.  Jurisdiction

In the case of any litigation, Underwriters accept the jurisdiction of the courts at their Seat of Administration for the entire Swiss Business, Seefeldstrasse 7, 8008 Zurich, or at the Swiss domicile of the Assured or Assignee.  Lloyd's Underwriters' Mandataire General for Switzerland is authorised to represent all the undersigned Underwriters in any litigation with the right for substitution in legal proceedings.

19.  Applicable Law

As far as this Insurance contains nothing to the contrary, the provisions of the Swiss Federal Law concerning the Insurance Contract of 2nd April, 1908 are applicable.
